About Me

It's time for something new.

My work history began in finance and transitioned to IT several years later. To be honest, never in a million years would I have thought I'd end up in tech, but looking back, there were signs.

As a kid, I remember taking apart small gadgets lying around the house because I was curious to see how things worked. My parents didn't understand my curiosity and labeled me as the destroyer. Although there’s nothing particularly interesting about the innards of a remote control, I loved seeing what was on the inside and how the pieces were connected.

As I got older, I was less interested in seeing the internal parts of objects and tapped into expressing my creative side. I wrote poems and short stories in my spare time and completed creative writing assignments for my peers. As I write this, I’m wondering how I got into finance. It was the family business, that's how. Both my parents worked in corporate America for many years.

After working in finance for a few years as a creative, work got boring and mundane; I hated it! I wanted out at all costs. On the commute home one evening, I saw an advertisement on the train for an IT program and decided to give it a try. I loved it in the beginning and after working in IT for 6 years, I’m ready for another transition.

I recently took a bootcamp course and began learning web development using front and back-end technologies. I learned in 6 months what some developers learn over the course of 20 years. It wasn’t easy, especially with a full time job, but I stuck with the program as it got more and more challenging because I started to love it and I see the potential to create something great. Even though I completed the bootcamp, I know learning and practicing what I've learned never ends. I'll continue learning and developing my skills because I have found a way to express my creativity through what I can code. Review my skills and projects I've worked on to see how I can benefit you.
